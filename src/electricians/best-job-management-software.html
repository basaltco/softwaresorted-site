Best Job Management Software for UK Electricians (2026)

Choosing the best job management software for electricians is one of the most important decisions a UK electrical business makes — and one of the hardest to reverse once you're embedded in a system. This guide covers the four tools worth considering, based on original research, verified user reviews, and real UK pricing. No sponsored content, no vendor relationships influencing the verdicts.

Quick Verdict

Tradify for most sole traders and small teams — straightforward, Android and iOS, clean Xero sync, fast to set up. Powered Now if you regularly produce EICRs, Minor Works certificates, or work under CIS — it's the only UK-native tool in this category and handles compliance properly. ServiceM8 only if your entire team is on iPhone and client automation is a priority. Jobber for larger teams of five or more who want a polished client portal and richer scheduling.

All four offer 14-day free trials with no card required. That's the right way to test them — run a few real jobs through before committing.

Why This Decision Matters

At £15–£50/mo per user, job management software is one of the bigger recurring costs in a small electrical business. More importantly, switching tools later is genuinely painful — users report migrations taking one to two full days with multiple staff relearning workflows mid-job. That's real money before you've sent a single invoice through the new system.

The other problem: most "best of" lists for this category are written by the vendors themselves, sponsored by the tools they review, or based on US and Australian markets where compliance requirements are completely different. CIS reverse charge, Making Tax Digital, EICR certificates — these are UK-specific requirements that most international tools don't handle natively. This guide is based on original research: verified user reviews from Capterra, G2, and Trustpilot, trade forum discussions, and real pricing checked directly with vendors.

The Four Tools Worth Your Time

Best all-rounder

Tradify — from £34/mo per user

Built for trades, used by tens of thousands of UK electricians. One place for quotes, jobs, scheduling, timesheets, and invoices. The quoting system lets you build a professional quote on-site and convert it to a live job the moment the client approves — no re-entering anything. Android and iOS both fully supported. Xero, Sage, and QuickBooks sync cleanly.

The AI quoting assistant on the Plus plan (£54/mo) auto-populates materials from imported supplier price lists — genuinely useful for anyone pricing multiple jobs against fluctuating cable and component costs. Most users are operational within an afternoon.

Catch: Every email Tradify sends carries a "sent from Tradify" footer. No compliance certificates. No offline mode.

Head-to-Head Comparisons

By team size

The Decision Framework

Sole trader, domestic work, certificates needed regularly → Powered Now Business (£15/mo). Best value in category. Sole trader or small team, domestic work, no certificates → Tradify Starter (£34/mo). Fast, clean, Xero sync included. Team with Android and iPhone users → Tradify. ServiceM8's Android app is too limited. Regular CIS subcontractor work or VAT reverse charge → Powered Now. Built for this natively. Whole team on iPhone, high volume short domestic jobs → ServiceM8. Client automation earns its price. 5+ person team wanting client portal and scheduling depth → Jobber. Better value at scale. Not sure where to start → Tradify's trial is the lowest-risk entry point.

What These Tools Don't Do

None of these replace a full estimating tool for larger commercial projects — if you're pricing from drawings, look at Countfire or Trimble. None handle payroll. None replace your accountant's Xero or Sage setup for VAT returns and year-end. They sit between the job and the invoice, handling the admin layer in between.

For businesses running more than 20 field staff, the schedulers on all four start to feel cramped. At that scale, Commusoft or SimPRO are worth a look — significantly more expensive and complex, but built for that volume.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does job management software cost for UK electricians?

Costs range from £15/mo (Powered Now Business, sole trader) to £170/mo+ for a five-person team on Tradify. Most electricians pay £34–£68/mo depending on team size. All four tools offer 14-day free trials with no card required.

Which software is best for EICR certificates?

Powered Now is the only tool in this category with built-in EICR certificates. It includes nearly 100 electrical compliance forms as standard, with digital signature capture on-site and automatic email delivery to clients. Tradify, ServiceM8, and Jobber don't include compliance certificates.

Does job management software handle CIS reverse charge?

Powered Now handles CIS natively — deduction calculations and the correct HMRC reverse charge invoice wording are built in. Tradify handles CIS through its Xero integration, which works but requires Xero to be configured correctly first. ServiceM8 and Jobber require manual workarounds.

Is there free job management software for electricians?

No fully-featured free option exists in this category. ServiceM8 has a free tier limited to 15 jobs per month — enough to test the software but not for active use. All four tools offer 14-day free trials. For very simple needs, Invoice Ninja or Wave handle basic invoicing for free but lack job management features.

Which software works offline for electricians?

Powered Now has the most mature full offline mode — the entire app works without signal and syncs when connectivity returns. Jobber added offline mode in January 2026. Tradify and ServiceM8 require internet connectivity to function.

Which tool integrates best with Xero?

Tradify's Xero integration is consistently rated the best in this category — invoices, bills, purchase orders, customers, and contacts all sync automatically. Jobber's Xero integration is also reliable. Powered Now's Xero sync works but requires more manual reconciliation. ServiceM8's integration is functional but the weakest of the four.
