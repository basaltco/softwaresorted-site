Best Job Management Software for UK Heating Engineers (2026)

Most software guides for tradespeople treat heating engineers the same as electricians or plumbers. The compliance requirements are different enough that this deserves its own guide. CP12 production, boiler service history, annual landlord recall — these are the workflows that matter and not every tool handles them.

This guide covers the four tools most commonly used by UK heating engineers — Tradify, Powered Now, ServiceM8, and Jobber — based on original research, verified user reviews from Capterra, G2, and Trustpilot, and real UK pricing confirmed directly with vendors. No sponsored content. Where a specialist tool is a better answer, we say so.

Quick Verdict

Powered Now for any Gas Safe heating engineer who produces CP12 landlord gas safety certificates — it is the only tool in this category with native CP12 production, automated annual service reminders, and boiler appliance history. Tradify for heating engineers whose work is primarily new installs, boiler swaps, and heating upgrades rather than ongoing service contracts — the most extensive gas certificate library of the four (boiler service records, commissioning certificates, gas breakdown forms), solid Xero sync, and the cleaner quoting workflow. ServiceM8 only if your entire team is on iPhone and you do high-volume short-duration boiler services with the gas form bundle add-on. All four offer 14-day free trials with no card required. If you produce CP12 certificates as a regular part of your business, start with Powered Now's trial.

Why This Decision Matters More for Heating Engineers

At £15–£50/mo per user, the subscription cost itself is not the critical variable. The real cost is switching. Users consistently report that migrating from one job management tool to another takes one to two full days of data export, import, and staff retraining — mid-job and mid-service-season. Choosing wrong once is an expensive lesson.

For heating engineers, there is a second problem that most comparison guides miss entirely: compliance paperwork is not occasional — it is the core product for a significant part of the business. A landlord with 20 rental properties needs a CP12 Landlord Gas Safety Record produced annually for every one of them. That is 20 legally required certificates per year, each containing prescribed information, each requiring digital delivery to the landlord and tenant within 28 days. Software that cannot produce a legally valid CP12 natively means you are running two systems — your job management tool for the commercial workflow, and a separate certificate app or paper pad for the document a landlord actually needs from you.

The annual service recall problem compounds this. Without automated reminders, you are manually tracking renewal dates across a spreadsheet or calendar. At 50 properties, this is manageable. At 150, it is a business risk — missed renewals, late certificates, landlord complaints. This is the workflow that separates general job management software from software built for heating engineers.

The UK Compliance Question

CP12 — Landlord Gas Safety Records. Under the Gas Safety (Installation and Use) Regulations 1998, landlords must have a Gas Safety Record completed annually for every gas appliance in a rented property by a Gas Safe registered engineer. The record must contain specific prescribed information: engineer name and Gas Safe registration number, appliance details, location, date, defects found, and whether the appliance was left safe to use. A job note or generic form is not a legally valid CP12. Powered Now is the only tool in this category that produces CP12 certificates as a native compliance form. Tradify has a Landlord Gas Safety Record template added to its UK forms library — but you should verify it meets the current Gas Safe Register format before relying on it professionally. ServiceM8 requires a third-party gas form bundle add-on. Jobber's CP12 capability has not been independently verified — do not assume it is available.

Boiler service records. Not legally mandated, but required for boiler warranty validity and expected by most commercial and landlord clients. A digital boiler service record that stores appliance history — previous service dates, parts replaced, readings taken — is the baseline expectation for any professional heating engineer. Tradify has a dedicated Boiler Service Record Sheet template and stores service history against each appliance. Powered Now stores appliance history against the customer record with automated renewal reminders attached.

Gas Safe registration. Every gas engineer must hold a current Gas Safe card for the appliance type being worked on. Software cannot replace this. But storing engineer Gas Safe card details and expiry dates — and alerting you before they lapse — is a feature worth checking for multi-engineer businesses. Powered Now and Tradify both allow engineer qualification records to be stored against the user profile.

Central heating commissioning certificates. Required on any new central heating system installation. Tradify includes a dedicated Central Heating Commissioning Certificate template in its UK forms library. This is relevant for engineers doing new-build or system replacement work alongside annual servicing.

Best for iOS teams

ServiceM8 — from ~£9/mo

Genuinely excellent software if your entire team is on iPhone. The client communication automation — automated arrival notifications, job completion confirmations, follow-up messages — is the most sophisticated in the category and useful for heating businesses doing high-volume boiler services with customer-facing timeslot management. Per-job pricing rather than per-user makes it cost-effective for high-volume, short-duration servicing work.

Gas certificate capability requires a third-party add-on: a gas form bundle available through the ServiceM8 form store that includes CP12 and related certificates. This adds a layer of setup complexity that Powered Now avoids entirely. The custom forms builder can produce additional certificate types. Worth the setup effort if the rest of ServiceM8's workflow suits your operation.

Catch: The Android app is a cut-down version — time tracking is excluded. One business on Trustpilot reviews switched their entire team from Android to iPhone to use ServiceM8 properly. No native CIS support. Gas certificate capability depends on a third-party form add-on rather than native compliance forms. Not recommended as the primary tool for any team with Android users.

Used by over 200,000 service businesses globally. Scheduling is the strongest in the category at five or more engineers, with route optimisation and real-time status tracking. Offline mode added in January 2026.

Catch: CP12 gas safety certificate production capability is not independently verified — do not assume this is available without confirming directly with Jobber before signing up. USD-first pricing jumps significantly from solo to team plans (~£31/mo to ~£134/mo adding a second person). No native CIS support. What These Tools Don't Do

None of these tools replace your Gas Safe registration — that is a regulatory requirement administered by the Gas Safe Register directly. None handle payroll. None replace your accountant's Xero or Sage setup for VAT returns and year-end accounts. They manage the admin layer between the job and the invoice: quoting, scheduling, job records, certificates, and payment chasing.

For businesses running more than 20 field engineers, all four schedulers start to feel constrained. At that scale, Commusoft — which is specifically popular with larger heating and plumbing businesses — or SimPRO are worth evaluating. Considerably more expensive and complex to implement, but built for high-volume multi-engineer operations and contract management at scale.
