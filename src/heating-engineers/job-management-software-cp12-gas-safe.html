Job Management Software for CP12 and Gas Safe Compliance: Which Tools Actually Handle It?

The question isn't whether your software can raise an invoice. Every tool in this category does that. The question is whether it produces a legally valid CP12 — and most job management tools, built for trades in general, either don't or do it via a workaround you won't find out about until you need it on-site.

This guide covers exactly what each of the four main tools does and doesn't do across the compliance requirements that apply to Gas Safe heating engineers in 2026.

The Compliance Requirements UK Heating Engineers Face

Before comparing the software, it is worth being precise about what "compliance" means here. There are four distinct requirements for most heating engineer businesses.

1. CP12 — Landlord Gas Safety Records

Under the Gas Safety (Installation and Use) Regulations 1998, landlords must have a Gas Safety Record completed annually for every gas appliance in a rented property. The engineer must hold a current Gas Safe card for the appliance type being worked on. The record must contain specific prescribed information: engineer name, Gas Safe registration number, appliance details, location, inspection date, defects identified, and whether the appliance was left safe to use. The landlord must receive a copy within 28 days, and a new tenant must receive it within 28 days of moving in.

A job note is not a CP12. A generic invoice is not a CP12. A completed PDF from an unstructured form builder may not be a CP12. The certificate must contain the required fields in a format that satisfies the Gas Safe Register's prescribed information requirements. This is where most general-purpose job management software falls short — and where heating engineers running landlord service contracts discover the gap at the worst possible moment.

2. Boiler Service Records

Not a legal requirement in the same way as a CP12, but boiler manufacturers require annual service records to maintain warranty validity. Landlords expect them. Commercial clients expect them. The record needs to capture appliance details, service date, readings taken, any parts replaced, and engineer sign-off. A tool that stores boiler service history against the appliance record — not just against a job — lets you produce a complete service history for any property on request. This matters when a landlord asks "when was the boiler last serviced and what was done" and you need an answer in 30 seconds, not 30 minutes of calendar searching.

3. Annual Service Recall

The CP12 expires annually. The boiler service is due annually. For a heating engineer managing 50 or 100 landlord properties, tracking those renewal dates manually is an operational risk. Miss a renewal, and a landlord has an illegal let. Chase it too late, and you lose the service to a competitor who reminded them first. Automated annual reminder workflows — where the software tracks each certificate expiry date and sends the client a reminder before it lapses — are not a nice-to-have for this business model. They are what makes the model scalable.

4. Central Heating Commissioning Certificates

Required on any new central heating installation. The certificate records the system specification, commissioning date, readings and pressure checks, and engineer details. This applies to new-build work, full system replacements, and boiler swaps where the full system is recommissioned. Not all four tools support this as a specific form.

What "Native" CP12 Production Actually Means

There is a meaningful difference between a tool that produces CP12s natively and one that provides a form template or a third-party add-on.

Native CP12 production means the certificate is generated from data already in the system. The customer's address, the appliance details, the engineer's Gas Safe registration number — these pull through from the service record automatically. The engineer completes the inspection fields on-site, captures a digital signature, and the formatted certificate is generated and emailed to the landlord in one step. The certificate format is pre-built to meet the Gas Safe Register's prescribed information requirements.

A form template means the certificate structure exists, but the engineer fills in the fields manually — typically repeating information already recorded elsewhere in the job. Whether the form output meets the precise Gas Safe Register prescribed information requirements depends on the template. You should verify this with the software provider and, if possible, with the Gas Safe Register directly before relying on it for compliance certificates.

A third-party add-on means the certificate capability was built by an external developer for the platform's form store, not by the platform itself. The quality depends on the third-party developer. The add-on may not be maintained as Gas Safe Register requirements change. It introduces an additional setup step and an additional cost.

The Annual Service Recall Problem

For a sole trader doing 30 boiler services a year, tracking CP12 renewal dates in a calendar is manageable. For an engineer running 80 to 150 landlord properties, it is not — and the failure mode is not just losing the service job. A landlord whose CP12 has lapsed is legally operating an unlicensed let. When that is your responsibility to track and remind, the liability sits partly on you.

Automated annual recall works like this in Powered Now: when a CP12 is issued, the system records the certificate date against the customer record. Ahead of the annual renewal date, it sends the landlord an automated reminder that their gas safety check is due. The landlord contacts you to rebook. The engineer visits, completes the inspection, issues the new CP12, and the cycle resets. No manual tracking. No missed renewals. No awkward "I forgot to remind you" conversations with a landlord whose tenants have been living with an expired certificate.

Jobber's recurring jobs feature provides a partial equivalent. You can set a job to repeat annually against a customer, and the Jobber client portal makes upcoming services visible to the client. It requires more manual configuration than Powered Now's certificate-linked reminder system, but it is a workable alternative for larger teams already committed to Jobber.

Tradify and ServiceM8 have no equivalent. If you use either of these tools, annual service recall requires a separate system — calendar, spreadsheet, or a standalone reminder tool like Gas Engineer Software's notification feature.

ServiceM8 and the Gas Form Bundle

ServiceM8's CP12 capability requires installing a third-party gas form bundle from the ServiceM8 form store. This adds CP12, CP14, and related gas certificates as custom forms within the app. The setup takes around 30 minutes and the forms are reasonably well-structured for the gas trade.

Two catches worth knowing: the form bundle is maintained by a third-party developer, not ServiceM8 directly. If Gas Safe Register requirements change, the bundle needs to be updated by that developer. And ServiceM8's Android app is a cut-down version — if any of your engineers are on Android, they cannot access the full workflow including time tracking, which affects the practicality of the whole setup.

ServiceM8 is a sensible option for an iOS-only heating team doing high-volume boiler services where the per-job pricing model suits the business. It is not the right answer for any team with Android devices or for engineers whose CP12 compliance is the primary business concern.

Frequently Asked Questions

What information must a CP12 contain to be legally valid?

Under the Gas Safety (Installation and Use) Regulations 1998, a Landlord Gas Safety Record (CP12) must include: the engineer's name and Gas Safe registration number; the date of inspection; the address of the property; a description and location of each appliance or flue inspected; any defects identified; whether the appliance was found to be immediately dangerous, at risk, or safe to use; and confirmation that all necessary work has been carried out. Omitting any of these fields means the certificate does not satisfy the legal requirement. A job note or general-purpose invoice cannot substitute for a CP12.

Is there a legal deadline for issuing a CP12 to a landlord?

Yes. The landlord must receive a copy of the Gas Safety Record within 28 days of the inspection. New tenants must receive a copy within 28 days of moving in, or before they move in if the inspection was carried out in advance of their tenancy. Existing tenants must receive a copy of each new Gas Safety Record within 28 days of the inspection. The landlord must also retain a copy for two years.

Does the software send the CP12 directly to the landlord?

Powered Now sends the completed certificate to the landlord directly from the app via email as part of the native CP12 workflow. The certificate is generated on-site, the engineer captures a digital signature, and the PDF is emailed automatically. Tradify's template approach generates a PDF that you send manually. ServiceM8's gas form bundle generates a certificate you send from the app. Delivery workflow varies between tools — confirm the process during the trial.

Can these tools store boiler appliance history across multiple service visits?

Powered Now stores boiler service history against the customer's appliance record — each service visit appends to the same appliance history, so you have a full service log accessible from any future visit. Tradify records job history at the customer level, so previous boiler service records are accessible as past jobs rather than as a structured appliance history. ServiceM8 and Jobber store job records at the customer level with no dedicated appliance-level history.

What happens if a heating engineer's Gas Safe registration lapses?

Carrying out gas work without a current Gas Safe registration is a criminal offence under the Gas Safety (Installation and Use) Regulations 1998. Any certificates issued during the lapsed period are invalid. Powered Now includes engineer qualification record storage with expiry alerts — worth setting up for any multi-engineer business. Tradify includes user profile fields for qualifications. For sole traders, a calendar reminder is the minimum; purpose-built qualification tracking is safer.

Does job management software replace the Gas Safe Register?

No. Gas Safe registration is a regulatory requirement administered by the Gas Safe Register directly — it is not something software can provide or substitute. Every engineer carrying out gas work must be Gas Safe registered for the relevant appliance categories and hold a current Gas Safe card. Software manages the job workflow, certificate production, and record-keeping around the work. It does not replace the registration itself.
