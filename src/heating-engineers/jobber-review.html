Quick Verdict

Jobber is the strongest scheduling and client management tool for heating businesses of five or more engineers. The client self-service portal — where maintenance contract customers can approve quotes, view their service history, and pay invoices without phoning the office — is genuinely useful for heating businesses running recurring annual service accounts. At five or more engineers, the team plan pricing becomes competitive and the scheduling tools start earning their keep.

The catches are significant for heating engineers specifically. CP12 gas safety certificate production capability could not be independently verified — do not assume it is available without confirming directly with Jobber before signing up. There is no CIS support. The pricing jump from solo (~£31/mo) to any team plan (~£134/mo) is steep. For sole traders or two-person operations, Jobber is poor value unless the client portal specifically solves a problem you have.

What Jobber Actually Does

Jobber is used by over 200,000 service businesses globally. Its strengths are scheduling at scale, client relationship management, and the self-service portal. The dispatch board gives a real-time view of all engineers across all active jobs, with drag-and-drop scheduling and route optimisation built in. At five or more engineers this becomes meaningfully useful; at two engineers it is more feature than you need.

The client portal is Jobber's most distinctive feature in this category. Clients — whether residential homeowners, landlords, or property managers — get their own login to a branded portal where they can approve quotes, track active jobs in real time, view their complete job history, and pay invoices online. For a heating business managing recurring annual service accounts with letting agents or large private landlords, this makes the client relationship self-service in a way no other tool here achieves.

Offline mode was added in January 2026. The recurring jobs feature allows jobs to be scheduled automatically on a repeat basis — useful for annual boiler services where you want the job to appear in the diary without manual creation each year.

The CP12 Question

CP12 Landlord Gas Safety Certificate production could not be independently verified. Jobber's website does not clearly describe UK gas compliance certificate capability, and we could not confirm it from publicly available product documentation. Before signing up to Jobber for a heating engineering business where CP12 production is required, contact Jobber directly and ask explicitly: "Does Jobber produce CP12 Landlord Gas Safety Records to the Gas Safe Register's prescribed information requirements?" Do not proceed on an assumption. If this capability is absent or requires workarounds, Powered Now is the tool to use instead.

How It Works on a Real Job

A property management company with 35 managed properties asks you to tender for their annual boiler service contract. Here is how Jobber handles that relationship once you have won the work:

You create the property management company as a client in Jobber and set up recurring annual service jobs for each property. Each engineer sees their allocated properties in their job list. The property manager gets a portal login — from there, they can see which services have been completed, which are scheduled, view job reports, and pay the monthly service contract invoice online without phoning the office.

When a job is completed, the engineer marks it done from their phone and the property manager sees the update in the portal in real time. The automated follow-up message goes to the property manager confirming completion. At month end, the batch invoice for all services that month is raised from the jobs, sent automatically, and paid via the portal. The property manager's experience is professional and self-contained.

The gap for this scenario is the CP12. If CP12 production is part of the contract — which it would be for residential properties — Jobber's capability on this needs to be confirmed directly before you quote the contract.

The Catch

CP12 capability is unverified. See the specific note above. Do not assume this is available before confirming with Jobber directly.

The pricing jump is steep. The Core plan at ~£31/mo covers one user. Adding a second person jumps you to the Connect plan at ~£134/mo — a 330% increase for one additional user. If you are a two-person operation, Jobber is roughly three times the cost of Powered Now Professional (£50/mo for two users) for largely equivalent functionality at that team size.

USD pricing adds uncertainty. Every pound/dollar exchange rate shift changes your monthly cost. In a year where GBP moves significantly against USD, your Jobber bill changes without Jobber changing their pricing. Budget with a buffer or confirm GBP pricing directly.

No CIS support. Heating engineers working under the Construction Industry Scheme need a different tool. Powered Now handles CIS natively. Tradify handles it through Xero. Jobber has no native CIS capability.

QuickBooks integration issues. Multiple Capterra reviewers report reliability problems with the Jobber/QuickBooks sync specifically. If QuickBooks is your accounting software, test the integration thoroughly during the trial.

What Real Users Say

Heating and plumbing businesses on Capterra consistently highlight the client portal as the differentiating feature: "Our letting agents log in themselves to check job status and download reports. We have cut the number of 'is it done yet?' calls by about 70%."

The pricing jump gets consistent criticism: "I went from the single-user plan thinking I'd add my apprentice and the cost jumped from £30 to over £130 a month. That should be clearer upfront." A second recurring theme on G2: "Jobber is built for US and Canadian service businesses. Some UK-specific things — like how VAT and CIS work — feel like afterthoughts."

The offline mode addition has been welcomed but with caveats: "The offline mode works for basic job completion but it's new and not as solid as tools that have had it for years. I wouldn't rely on it in a basement without testing it first."

Engineers doing CIS subcontractor work. No native CIS support. Powered Now handles this; Tradify handles it through Xero.
