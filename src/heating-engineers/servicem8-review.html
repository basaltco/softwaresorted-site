Quick Verdict

ServiceM8 is genuinely excellent field service software — for the right team. The client communication automation is the most sophisticated in this category: automated arrival notifications, on-my-way texts, job completion confirmations, and follow-up messages run without you lifting a finger. The per-job pricing model can make it cost-effective for high-volume boiler servicing businesses. For iOS-only heating teams doing largely domestic work, it is a serious option.

The hard constraint is Android. ServiceM8's Android app is a significantly cut-down version of the iOS app — time tracking is excluded entirely. Any team with a mix of Android and iPhone cannot use ServiceM8 properly. A second constraint for heating engineers specifically: gas certificate capability requires a third-party add-on from the ServiceM8 form store, not a native feature. And there are no automated annual service reminders. Know these before starting a trial.

What ServiceM8 Actually Does

ServiceM8 approaches field service management from a client communication angle more than any other tool in this category. The customer experience — from booking confirmation through arrival notification to job completion message — is automated and polished in a way that feels more like a consumer app than a trades tool. For heating businesses where customer communication quality is a competitive differentiator (premium domestic boiler service, landlord portfolio management where the letting agent expects professionalism), this is genuinely useful.

The scheduling and dispatch tools are solid. Jobs appear on an interactive map, engineers are assigned and notified from the same screen, and real-time status updates flow back as engineers update job progress. The per-job pricing model — where you pay per completed job rather than per user — works in favour of heating teams doing high volumes of short-duration boiler services where the per-user model becomes expensive relative to job count.

The custom form builder allows you to create structured forms for any type of documentation. This is how gas certificates are produced in ServiceM8 — not as native forms, but as custom forms built using the form builder or installed from the third-party form store.

Gas Certificate Capability

Gas certificates are not a native ServiceM8 feature. To produce CP12 Landlord Gas Safety Records and related gas certificates, you need to install a third-party gas form bundle from the ServiceM8 form store. This is maintained by an external developer — not ServiceM8 — and adds CP12, CP14 (Gas Warning Notice), and related certificates as custom forms within the app.

The setup takes around 30 minutes and the forms are adequately structured for the gas trade. But there are two practical risks: the bundle is maintained by a third party, so updates when Gas Safe Register requirements change depend on that developer staying current; and the forms require the engineer to manually fill in all fields rather than auto-populating from existing customer and boiler records. Test the gas form bundle during the trial period and check when it was last updated before committing.

There are no automated annual service reminders in ServiceM8. For heating engineers managing landlord service portfolios where annual CP12 renewal tracking is core, a separate system is needed alongside ServiceM8.

How It Works on a Real Job

A landlord books five annual boiler services across their portfolio for next Tuesday. Here is how this runs in ServiceM8 for an iOS-only team:

Jobs are created and assigned to the engineer's diary from the office. ServiceM8 automatically sends the tenants booking confirmation texts and emails. On Tuesday morning, the engineer gets their job list on their iPhone — five jobs, with addresses, access notes, and appliance details from previous visits. Before each visit, the automated "on my way" notification goes to the tenant.

At each property, the engineer opens the job, installs the gas form bundle certificate, completes the CP12 fields manually (customer and boiler details need to be re-entered as they do not auto-populate from the system), captures a digital signature, and the certificate is saved against the job. On completion, the customer receives an automated notification that the service is done. The engineer adds time and parts used, marks the job complete, and moves to the next property.

Back at base, invoices are raised from the completed jobs. The client communication — booking, reminders, arrival, completion — ran entirely without manual effort. That automation is what ServiceM8 does better than any other tool here. The gap is the annual recall: when these same properties need their CP12 renewed next year, there is no automated reminder going to the landlord. That date tracking is manual.

The Catch

Android users cannot use ServiceM8 properly. The ServiceM8 Android app is a significantly reduced version of the iOS app. Time tracking — a core feature for any business billing labour by the hour — is excluded from Android entirely. This is not a minor limitation. One business on Trustpilot switched their entire team of engineers from Android to iPhone specifically to use ServiceM8. For any heating team with a mix of devices, or any sole trader on Android, ServiceM8 is not a viable full solution.

Gas certificates require a third-party add-on, not a native feature. See the gas certificate section above. The setup complexity and third-party maintenance dependency are worth understanding before committing.

No CIS support. If you do work under the Construction Industry Scheme as a subcontractor, ServiceM8 has no native CIS handling. Powered Now covers this natively. Tradify handles it through Xero. ServiceM8 requires manual workarounds that most accountants will not be satisfied with.

No offline mode. ServiceM8 requires internet connectivity to function. Engineers cannot complete jobs, produce certificates, or log time without signal.

QuickBooks integration issues. For the minority of UK heating businesses using QuickBooks, multiple Capterra reviews report reliability problems with the QuickBooks sync. If QuickBooks is your accounting software, test the integration carefully during the trial.

Who This Is NOT For

Any team with Android users. The Android app cannot do time tracking. This is not a workaround situation — time tracking is architecturally absent from the Android version. Mixed-device teams cannot use ServiceM8 properly.

Heating engineers who produce regular CP12 certificates. Gas certificate capability depends on a third-party add-on with manual field entry. Powered Now is the practical choice for any business where CP12 compliance is a core workflow.

Engineers doing CIS subcontractor work. No native CIS support, no practical workaround. Use Powered Now or Tradify with Xero.

Engineers working regularly in areas with poor signal. No offline capability. Any job, certificate, or time log requires internet connectivity.

Businesses running annual service contract portfolios. No automated annual recall reminders. Managing renewal dates for 50+ properties requires a separate system alongside ServiceM8.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does ServiceM8 work for heating engineers in the UK?

ServiceM8 is used by UK heating engineers, primarily iOS-only teams. It supports UK pricing and integrates with Xero and QuickBooks. Gas certificate capability requires installing a third-party gas form bundle from the form store — it is not a native feature. There is no CIS support and no automated annual service reminders. It is best suited to iOS-only teams doing high-volume boiler services where the client communication automation and per-job pricing model are priorities.

Does ServiceM8 produce CP12 gas safety certificates?

Via a third-party gas form bundle add-on from the ServiceM8 form store, yes. This bundle includes CP12, CP14, and related gas certificates as custom forms within the app. The bundle is maintained by an external developer, not ServiceM8 directly. Fields do not auto-populate from existing customer records — engineers fill them in manually each time. Powered Now includes CP12 production as a native compliance form with auto-population from existing records.

Does ServiceM8 work on Android for heating engineers?

The ServiceM8 Android app is a cut-down version that excludes time tracking. This is not a minor missing feature — for a heating business billing labour by the hour, time tracking from the field is essential. Any team with Android engineers cannot use ServiceM8 as a full solution. Tradify and Powered Now both have full feature parity on Android and iOS.

How much does ServiceM8 cost for a heating business?

ServiceM8 charges per completed job rather than per user. Plans range from a free tier (15 jobs/month) to roughly £9/mo (50 jobs), £29/mo (150 jobs), £59/mo (500 jobs), and £119/mo (unlimited). Prices are approximate GBP conversions from AUD. The per-job model can be cost-effective for high-volume boiler servicing businesses — at 150 jobs per month on the Growing plan, cost per job is under £0.20. At lower volumes, per-user tools like Powered Now are competitively priced.

Does ServiceM8 handle CIS for heating engineers?

No. ServiceM8 has no native CIS support. If you do subcontractor work under the Construction Industry Scheme, ServiceM8 requires manual workarounds that create accounting complexity. Powered Now handles CIS natively. Tradify handles it through a correctly configured Xero integration.
