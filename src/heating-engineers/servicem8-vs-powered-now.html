ServiceM8 vs Powered Now for Heating Engineers (2026)

This is not a close comparison for most heating engineers. Powered Now covers the compliance requirements — native CP12, automated annual reminders, full offline capability, CIS — that ServiceM8 does not address natively. ServiceM8's advantage is a more sophisticated client communication workflow and cheaper per-job pricing at high volumes. If you are on Android, or if compliance documentation is the bottleneck, read no further — use Powered Now.

Quick Verdict

Powered Now for the vast majority of Gas Safe heating engineers. Native CP12 production, automated annual service reminders, full Android support, full offline capability, and CIS handling make it the practical choice for compliance-focused businesses. Significantly cheaper at small team sizes.

ServiceM8 only for iOS-only heating teams doing high-volume boiler services where the automated client communication workflow is a genuine operational priority and the gas form bundle setup is acceptable. Not a compliance tool — it is a field automation tool that can approximate compliance with extra setup.

The One Area Where ServiceM8 Leads

ServiceM8's client communication automation is better than Powered Now's. The sequence of automated messages — booking confirmation, appointment reminder, on-my-way notification, job completion message, follow-up — runs without manual intervention and is more configurable than anything Powered Now offers. For heating businesses doing high-volume domestic boiler services where customer experience is a differentiator, this is real. It is not a compliance advantage; it is a service delivery advantage.

If your operation is primarily domestic boiler services on iPhone where you want customers to feel like they are dealing with a premium operation — and you are willing to set up the gas form bundle — ServiceM8 is worth trialling alongside Powered Now. Most Gas Safe heating engineers will find the compliance gaps outweigh the communication automation, but it is worth knowing what ServiceM8 does well before dismissing it.

Who Should Choose Which

Choose Powered Now if…

  • You produce CP12 certificates regularly
  • You manage annual service contracts for landlords
  • Any engineer on your team is on Android
  • You work in areas with unreliable signal

  • You want the cheaper option at any team size

Choose ServiceM8 if…

  • Entire team is on iPhone — no exceptions
  • High-volume domestic boiler services = many jobs per month
  • Automated customer texts and notifications are a priority
  • You will set up and maintain the gas form bundle
  • CP12s are a secondary rather than primary workflow

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ServiceM8 or Powered Now better for Gas Safe heating engineers?

Powered Now for the majority. Native CP12 production, automated annual service reminders, full Android support, offline capability, and CIS handling are all native features that ServiceM8 does not provide without workarounds or add-ons. ServiceM8's client communication automation is the one area where it is genuinely better — relevant only for iOS-only teams where that workflow is a priority.

Can ServiceM8 replace Powered Now for CP12 compliance?

Not easily. ServiceM8's CP12 capability requires a third-party gas form bundle add-on with manual field entry. Powered Now's CP12 is a native compliance form that auto-populates from existing customer and boiler records, with automated delivery to the landlord and annual renewal reminders. The setup and maintenance complexity of the ServiceM8 approach means it is not a practical substitute for a business where CP12 compliance is a core workflow.

Is Powered Now cheaper than ServiceM8 for heating engineers?

It depends on job volume. Powered Now charges per user (£15/mo sole trader, £25/mo per user). ServiceM8 charges per completed job (~£9/mo for 50 jobs, ~£29/mo for 150 jobs). For a sole trader doing 50 boiler services per month, ServiceM8 at ~£9/mo is cheaper than Powered Now at £15/mo. For a two-person team, Powered Now at £50/mo is likely cheaper than ServiceM8 at similar job volumes. Run the numbers at your actual job count.
