Quick Verdict

Tradify is the strongest choice for heating engineers whose work is primarily new boiler installations, system upgrades, and central heating replacements — where rapid quoting, clean Xero sync, and solid scheduling are the daily bottlenecks. Its gas certificate library is the most extensive of the four tools reviewed: boiler service records, central heating commissioning certificates, landlord gas safety records, gas breakdown forms, and oil firing reports are all included as UK-specific templates.

The catches matter for service-contract engineers. There are no automated annual service reminders — if you manage a landlord portfolio where CP12 renewal tracking is core to your business, you will need a separate system for that. There is no offline mode. And the Landlord Gas Safety Record is a template rather than the native compliance form that Powered Now provides. For install-led businesses these gaps are minor; for service-contract businesses they are dealbreakers. Know which business you are before trialling.

What Tradify Actually Does

Tradify is built around the trade workflow: quote fast, win the job, schedule it, complete the work, get paid. It does this better than any other tool in this category. The quoting system is where most heating engineers notice it first — build a quote from a saved price list or imported parts catalogue, send it from your phone while still on-site with the customer, and convert it to a live job in one tap when approved. No re-entering anything. No going back to the office to raise a quote on a desktop.

The mobile app is the same experience on Android and iOS — not a cut-down version — which matters for heating teams where engineers are on a mix of devices. Job costing updates in real time as you quote, so margin is visible before you send anything. The Xero, Sage, and QuickBooks integrations are clean and reliable — Tradify's Xero connection is consistently rated the best in this category across Capterra and G2 reviews.

For heating engineers specifically, the UK gas certificate library is the headline feature that distinguishes Tradify from other general-purpose tools. This is not a single form — it is a library of gas-trade-specific templates added for the UK market, which means any Gas Safe engineer is producing the right paperwork from within the same system they use for quoting and invoicing.

Tradify's Gas Certificate Library

Tradify includes the following gas certificates and forms as UK templates within the app:

  • Landlord Gas Safety Record — the CP12 equivalent for rental properties
  • Boiler Service Record Sheet — for annual and ad hoc boiler servicing
  • Central Heating Commissioning Certificate — required on new installations
  • Gas Breakdown and Service Certificate — for emergency call-outs and service visits
  • Gas Installation Safety Record — for new gas appliance installations
  • Service Maintenance Record — for ongoing maintenance agreements
  • CP14 — Gas Warning Notice — for unsafe appliances that cannot be made safe
  • CP42 — Commercial Catering Gas Safety Certificate — for commercial kitchen work
  • CD11 — Oil Firing Servicing and Commissioning Report — for oil boiler engineers

This is the most comprehensive gas certificate library of the four tools reviewed. Important caveat: the Landlord Gas Safety Record is a template with the required fields — it is not a native compliance form with auto-populated prescribed data and automated delivery in the way Powered Now's CP12 is. Verify the template output meets the current Gas Safe Register prescribed information requirements before using it for compliance certificates.

How It Works on a Real Job

A homeowner calls about a boiler replacement — their current boiler failed overnight and they want a quote today. Here is the Tradify workflow from that call to a signed commissioning certificate in the customer's inbox:

You create the customer record on your phone during the call. You build a quote from your boiler replacement price template — labour, new boiler, flue kit, filter, and any extras — and send it by email within three minutes of hanging up. The customer approves it online. You convert it to a job with one tap, assign it to your diary for tomorrow, and the job appears in your engineer's schedule automatically.

On installation day, the engineer opens the job on their phone, sees all the job details and the quote line items, and logs time as the job progresses. When the installation is complete, they open the Central Heating Commissioning Certificate template, fill in the system details, readings, and sign-off information, and the completed certificate is saved against the job and sent to the customer as a PDF.

Back at the office, you raise the invoice from the completed job record in about two minutes. It pushes to Xero, the customer gets a professional invoice, and the payment reminder goes out automatically if they have not settled within your terms. That job — from call to certificate to paid invoice — runs through one system without double-entering anything.

The gap is the annual service reminder. Tradify records the job history, but there is no mechanism to automatically contact the customer when their next annual service is due. If this homeowner's new boiler needs an annual service in 12 months, you will need to track that date in a calendar or separate tool.

The Catch

No automated annual service reminders. This is the significant limitation for heating engineers with service contract work. Tradify records service history against customers, but there is no way to set an automated "your annual boiler service is due" reminder. For engineers managing landlord portfolios of 20, 50, or 100 properties, this means running a separate system — calendar, spreadsheet, or a tool like Google Contacts with reminder dates. Powered Now solves this natively. If annual recall is core to your business model, this alone may be the deciding factor.

The Landlord Gas Safety Record is a template, not a native compliance form. The certificate structure is there and the required fields are included, but the data does not auto-populate from existing customer and boiler records the way Powered Now's CP12 does. Engineers fill it in manually each time. Before using it for compliance certificates, verify the template output meets the Gas Safe Register's prescribed information requirements — this is your professional responsibility, not Tradify's.

No offline mode. Tradify requires an internet connection to function. For heating engineers working in older properties, plant rooms, and rural areas with unreliable signal, this is an operational constraint. Certificates cannot be produced or signed without connectivity. If you regularly work in signal-poor locations, Powered Now's offline capability is a meaningful difference.

The email footer. Every quote, invoice, and job confirmation sent from Tradify includes a "sent from Tradify" branded footer. Multiple Capterra reviewers — across trades, not just heating — have flagged this as unprofessional for commercial and estate agent clients. The workaround is to download documents and send them from your own email address, which removes a significant part of what you are paying for.

Who This Is NOT For

Engineers working regularly in areas with poor signal. Tradify requires internet to function. No offline certificates, no offline job completion. If signal-poor sites are common — older properties, rural locations, basement plant rooms — this is an operational constraint, not just an inconvenience.

Commercial gas engineers needing template-level CP12 validation. The Landlord Gas Safety Record template needs to be verified against current Gas Safe Register prescribed information requirements before professional use.
