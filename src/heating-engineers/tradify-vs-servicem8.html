Tradify vs ServiceM8 for Heating Engineers (2026)

For most heating engineer teams, this comparison ends at one question: do you have Android users? If yes, Tradify. ServiceM8's Android app excludes time tracking — a fundamental feature for any business billing labour by the hour. That single constraint eliminates ServiceM8 for the majority of mixed-device UK heating teams.

If your team is iPhone throughout, the comparison becomes more nuanced. ServiceM8's client communication automation is genuinely better than Tradify's, and its per-job pricing can work out cheaper for high-volume boiler servicing. Here is how the rest of the comparison runs.

Quick Verdict

Tradify for any team with Android users, any engineer who needs a gas certificate library without a third-party add-on, or any business doing CIS subcontractor work. It is the straightforward answer for the majority of UK heating engineer teams.

ServiceM8 only for iOS-only teams where the client communication automation — automated texts and notifications at every job stage — is a priority, and where the gas form bundle setup is worth the extra configuration. High-volume boiler servicing businesses on iPhone where the per-job pricing model suits the job volume.

Pricing: Which Is Cheaper?

ServiceM8's per-job pricing model means the comparison depends on job volume, not team size. For a sole trader doing 50 boiler services per month, ServiceM8 costs roughly £9/mo against Tradify's £34/mo — ServiceM8 wins on price at this volume. At 150 jobs per month, ServiceM8 jumps to approximately £29/mo — still below Tradify for a solo engineer. For a two-person team sharing Tradify at £68/mo, ServiceM8 stays cheaper unless job volume is very high. At high volumes (500+ jobs/month), ServiceM8 at ~£59/mo (Premium) competes with larger Tradify team plans.

The per-job model benefits high-volume operations. The per-user model is more predictable for planning. Both have 14-day free trials.

Who Should Choose Which

Choose Tradify if…

  • Any team member is on Android
  • You want gas certificates without a third-party add-on
  • Fast quoting is the primary daily bottleneck
  • Your accountant relies on a clean Xero sync

  • You want predictable fixed monthly costs

  • Your entire team is on iPhone — no exceptions

  • Client communication automation (texts, arrival alerts) is a priority
  • You are willing to configure the gas form bundle during setup

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Tradify or ServiceM8 better for heating engineers?

Tradify for the majority. Any team with Android users, any engineer needing a gas certificate library without an add-on, and any business doing CIS work should choose Tradify. ServiceM8 is the better option only for iPhone-only teams where the automated client communication workflow and per-job pricing model are worth the setup complexity and third-party gas form dependency.

Does ServiceM8 produce gas safety certificates for heating engineers?

Via a third-party gas form bundle add-on from the ServiceM8 form store. This includes CP12 and related certificates as custom forms. The bundle is maintained by an external developer, requires setup, and fields do not auto-populate from existing customer records. Tradify includes gas certificates as native UK templates without requiring an add-on. Neither tool provides automated annual service reminders — Powered Now is the tool for that workflow.

Can ServiceM8 handle Android devices for heating engineers?

Not fully. ServiceM8's Android app excludes time tracking. For heating businesses where engineers log hours on-site — billing by the hour for service calls, installation time, or call-outs — the Android version cannot do the core job. Any team with Android users should use Tradify or Powered Now instead.
