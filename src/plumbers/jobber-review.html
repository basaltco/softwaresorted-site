Quick Verdict

Jobber is a polished, well-supported platform used by over 200,000 service businesses globally. For UK plumbers it handles the core workflow well — quoting, scheduling, invoicing, client communications — and the mobile app is genuinely good on both Android and iOS. It added offline mode in January 2026, removing one of its biggest previous limitations.

The catches are real: it's a Canadian product with USD-first pricing, no native Gas Safe certificates or CIS support, and the Core-to-Connect pricing jump makes it expensive for small teams. For UK plumbing businesses whose accountant uses Xero, Tradify is cheaper and cleaner for teams under five. For larger heating and plumbing businesses that want a polished client maintenance portal and richer scheduling, Jobber earns its place. For anything requiring Gas Safe compliance, Powered Now is the right tool regardless of team size.

What Jobber Actually Does

Jobber covers the full job lifecycle: online booking, quoting, scheduling, crew dispatch, time tracking, invoicing, and payment collection. The client hub is a genuine differentiator — customers get a self-service portal to approve quotes, view job history, pay invoices, and request new work without calling the office. For plumbing businesses serving property managers or facilities companies with multiple sites, a professional client portal that tracks job history across multiple properties is worth real money in client retention.

The scheduling calendar is strong at five or more engineers — drag-and-drop, crew availability view, and route optimisation that adjusts in real time as the day changes. Assigning jobs to the nearest available plumber is genuinely better here than in Tradify or Powered Now. The AI Marketing Suite generates client follow-up campaigns from simple prompts — useful for heating maintenance contract renewals and seasonal boiler service reminders. Offline mode added in January 2026 means jobs, notes, and photos cache locally and sync when signal returns, though it's newer than Powered Now's mature offline implementation.

How It Works on a Real Job

A facilities manager for a commercial property sends an email requesting an annual boiler service and Water Regulations compliance check across three sites. Here's the Jobber workflow:

You create the jobs in Jobber, assigning them to the engineers' calendars across three days. The client automatically receives booking confirmations with links to their self-service portal where they can view all pending and completed jobs. Engineers open Jobber on-site — offline if needed — view all job details, log time, take and annotate photos. When each job is complete, the client portal pushes the invoice automatically. They can pay online and download the invoice directly from the portal without calling the office.

That workflow is smooth for maintenance contract clients who value the professional portal experience. The gap: any compliance documentation — boiler service record, Gas Safety Record, Water Regulations compliance certificate — still needs producing separately. Jobber has no native compliance certificate capability for UK plumbers.

The Catch

QuickBooks integration drops line items. The most consistent complaint across Capterra, G2, and GetApp — approximately 2% of line items drop during sync, auto-sync breaks without warning, and manual reconciliation becomes routine. Several users have abandoned the integration entirely. If your accountant relies on QuickBooks for clean automatic reconciliation, test this thoroughly during the trial. Jobber's Xero integration is significantly more reliable.

Sharp pricing jump at two users. Core to Connect goes from ~£31/mo to ~£134/mo the moment you add one employee. Tradify at £68/mo for two users is materially cheaper. Jobber's value only clearly overtakes at five or more users.

No Gas Safe certificates, no CIS. Canadian product, UK compliance is not a development priority. Gas Safety Records, CP12 landlord certificates, Legionella templates, CIS reverse charge invoicing — none are natively supported. For domestic-only plumbing this may not matter. For any gas-registered or subcontractor work it's a meaningful gap.

Google Calendar sync is 24-hour lag. Not real-time. Engineers who rely on Google Calendar for personal scheduling end up managing two calendars manually.

How It Compares

Against Tradify: Tradify is cheaper for small teams and has better Xero integration. Jobber has stronger scheduling at larger team sizes, a richer client portal, and AI marketing tools. For a sole trader or small team using Xero, Tradify wins. For a five-plus person heating and plumbing business that wants polished client experience, Jobber is competitive.

Against Powered Now: No contest on UK compliance. Powered Now handles Gas Safe certificates, Legionella, CIS, MTD, and offline working natively. Jobber wins on scheduling sophistication and client portal. They serve different priorities.

The Tradesman's Math

At Core (~£31/mo) for a sole trader: save roughly 37 minutes of admin per week at £50/hr to break even. The sharper comparison: Tradify Starter at £34/mo gives similar core functionality with better Xero integration for roughly the same price. Jobber's advantage emerges at larger team sizes and for businesses that genuinely use the client portal and scheduling optimisation features — both of which are relevant for heating and plumbing maintenance contract businesses.

Who This Is NOT For

Teams of 2–4 on a budget. The Core to Connect jump makes Jobber expensive for small crews. Tradify's per-user model is cheaper at that team size.

Anyone whose accountant relies on QuickBooks auto-reconciliation. The sync issues are well-documented. Test it thoroughly during the trial.

Gas Safe registered plumbers needing compliance certificates. Gas Safety Records, CP12 certificates — not in Jobber. Powered Now is the answer.

Subcontractors working under CIS. No native reverse charge invoicing. Manual workarounds needed every time. Powered Now handles this properly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Jobber available in the UK for plumbers?

Yes. Jobber is available in the UK. However it's a Canadian product and UK compliance features — Gas Safe certificates, CIS, Making Tax Digital — are not natively supported. It suits UK plumbers doing domestic or commercial work where compliance documentation isn't required from the job management tool.

Why does Jobber get expensive for small plumbing teams?

Jobber's Core plan (~£31/mo) covers one user. Adding a second person requires upgrading to Connect (~£134/mo) — a significant jump. For teams of two to four people, Tradify's per-user model (£34/mo per person) is considerably cheaper. Jobber's team plan only becomes competitive at five or more users.

Does Jobber integrate with Xero for plumbing businesses?

Yes, and the Xero integration is reliable. Jobber's QuickBooks integration is where documented problems exist — line items dropping during sync and auto-sync breaking. If your accountant uses QuickBooks, test the integration thoroughly during the trial before committing.

How does Jobber compare to Tradify for UK plumbers?

Tradify is cheaper for small teams and has better Xero integration. Jobber has stronger scheduling, a client self-service portal, and AI marketing tools — relevant for maintenance contract plumbing businesses.
