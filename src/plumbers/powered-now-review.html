How It Works on a Real Job

A letting agent requests an annual gas safety check (CP12) on a rental property. Here's the Powered Now workflow from that request to a signed certificate in the landlord's inbox:

You create the job record in Powered Now, scheduling it to your diary. Your engineer opens the job on their Android or iPhone on the day — the app works offline from the moment they leave the depot if connectivity is uncertain. On-site, they complete the boiler service checks, then open the CP12 form within the same Powered Now job. They fill in all required safety fields: flue gas pressure readings, safe working clearances, ventilation adequacy, appliance condition. The tenant signs digitally on the engineer's phone screen.

Powered Now automatically generates a professionally formatted CP12 certificate and emails it directly to the landlord's email address. The engineer marks the job complete. Back in the office, you raise the invoice from the completed job record in under two minutes — it pushes to Xero, Sage, or QuickBooks, and the payment reminder goes out automatically if the invoice isn't settled within your terms.

That single workflow — compliance certificate, client document, and invoice, all in one app — is where Powered Now earns its subscription for any plumber doing regular gas work. The alternative is producing the CP12 on a separate system, emailing it manually, and reconciling it later. Multiply that by the number of gas jobs a week, and the time saving is immediate.

The Catch

No lower-cost field-only user tier. Every user pays the Professional rate of £25/mo. For a business with several field engineers who mainly view jobs and log time — rather than admin staff who manage quoting and invoicing — this adds up. Tradify's model is the same, but it's worth flagging for businesses where there's a clear distinction between office and field roles.

Xero integration requires more attention than Tradify's. Multiple Capterra reviewers have noted the Xero sync works but is less fully automated than Tradify's connection — some reconciliation is manual. If your accountant works primarily in Xero and expects everything to appear without intervention, test the integration carefully during the trial.

Quoting UI has more steps than Tradify. Building and sending a quote in Powered Now involves more taps than in Tradify, particularly for straightforward jobs from a price list. For businesses where quoting speed is the primary bottleneck rather than compliance documentation, this is worth experiencing in the trial.

Setup takes slightly longer. The broader feature set — compliance library, offline sync configuration, CIS setup — means initial setup takes more time than Tradify. Most users report being operational within a day, but it's not an afternoon job for anyone configuring the compliance forms properly.
