Quick Verdict

ServiceM8 is genuinely excellent software — clean, fast, and packed with automation features that the competition hasn't matched. The client communication tools are genuinely valuable for plumbing businesses doing high volumes of domestic call-outs: automated "on my way" texts reduce no-shows, arrival window notifications set expectations, and job completion follow-ups speed up payment. But there is one constraint that matters more than anything else: Android users get a stripped-down app. Time tracking is iOS-only. Several features available on iPhone either don't appear or work differently on Android. One Capterra reviewer reported switching their entire team from Android to iPhone to use ServiceM8 properly.

If everyone is on iPhone — and you can guarantee that stays true as you hire — ServiceM8 is a strong option for domestic plumbing call-out work. If not, Tradify is the more practical choice. And if you do any gas work that requires Gas Safe compliance certificates, neither ServiceM8 nor Tradify is the right tool — Powered Now is.

What ServiceM8 Actually Does

ServiceM8 is an Australian-built field service management platform that covers the full job lifecycle: online booking, quoting, job assignment, scheduling, real-time field updates, invoicing, and payment collection. The automation layer is what sets it apart. ServiceM8 can automatically send clients an "on my way" text when an engineer leaves, a job completion summary when work finishes, and follow-up messages at configurable intervals. For plumbing businesses doing high volumes of domestic call-outs — drain blockages, leak repairs, boiler faults — that level of automated client communication reduces admin significantly and is more sophisticated than anything Tradify or Powered Now offer in this area.

The custom forms builder is the other headline feature. Build detailed site documentation forms — risk assessments, job-specific checklists, client sign-offs — from scratch using a drag-and-drop interface. For plumbing businesses with specific site condition requirements or client approval workflows, it's significantly more flexible than Tradify's basic forms. Assets management lets you track installed equipment at client sites — useful for maintenance contracts covering boilers, water softeners, and other installed plant.

The scheduling board is drag-and-drop with real-time engineer location visibility, colour-coded job statuses, and the ability to reassign jobs instantly. Jobs cache locally while offline and sync when signal returns — providing a degree of offline resilience, though it's not the same as Powered Now's full offline mode.

Pricing is per account, not per user — meaning unlimited staff can be added at any tier, which compares favourably to Tradify's per-user model for larger teams. Jobs are counted per completed job. The catch is that a busy month costs more than a quiet one, which complicates cashflow forecasting. At 200+ jobs per month, Tradify's flat per-user rate becomes more economical.

Additional costs: SMS credits charged separately (10p per SMS on lower tiers), the ServiceM8 phone system is an additional ~£19/mo, and extra file storage is paid on top. One Capterra reviewer reported that add-on pricing had changed between their initial quote and when they attempted to activate the service — confirm all costs in writing before committing to any implementation.

How It Works on a Real Job

A property management company calls about an emergency call-out — a burst pipe in a commercial unit. Here's how ServiceM8 handles it on iOS:

The call can come through ServiceM8's phone integration, which logs it automatically and creates a job record. You assign it to your nearest available engineer on the scheduling board, who receives an instant notification on their iPhone. ServiceM8 automatically sends the client a booking confirmation text. When your engineer leaves, another automated text goes to the client: "Your plumber James is on his way, estimated arrival 2:15pm." On-site, the engineer opens the job on iPhone, views all details, logs time, takes photos, and fills in any custom forms. When the work is done, the client signs off digitally. ServiceM8 generates the invoice automatically and sends it with a card payment link. The client pays online.

That workflow is where ServiceM8 earns its reputation. The gap for UK plumbers is the compliance layer: no Gas Safe certificates, no CP12 records, no Legionella templates. Those still require a separate system. And if any engineer is on Android, the on-site experience is substantially reduced.

The Catch

Android is a genuine operational problem. The Android app is significantly reduced compared to iOS. Time tracking is not available on Android at all — it's iOS-only. Multiple reviewers on Trustpilot and Capterra have flagged this. One Capterra reviewer reported switching their entire team from Android to iPhone, with ServiceM8 contributing to the device cost. A Trustpilot reviewer who moved from iPhone to Android wrote that the Android app had "barely any functions that the Apple one does." This is not a temporary limitation — it reflects ServiceM8's iOS-first development priorities.

No Gas Safe compliance certificates. ServiceM8 cannot produce Gas Safety Records, CP12 landlord certificates, or any Gas Safe compliance documentation. No Legionella risk assessment templates. No CIS reverse charge invoicing native support. ServiceM8 is an Australian product and UK-specific compliance is not a core development priority. For any Gas Safe-registered plumber, this is a material gap.

Per-job pricing becomes expensive at volume. At 200+ jobs per month across a team, costs climb significantly. Tradify at £34/user/month is cheaper at higher job volumes. Budget carefully if your job count varies seasonally.

Recurring jobs have a scheduling quirk. Multiple Software Advice reviewers note that recurring jobs are calculated on 24-hour periods rather than working hours, causing them to display incorrectly in the calendar for jobs spanning multiple days. A minor but persistent issue for maintenance contract plumbers.

How It Compares

Against Tradify: ServiceM8's client automation and custom forms are more powerful. Tradify's Android support, Xero integration, and flat pricing are more practical for most UK plumbing teams. The decision comes down to device consistency — if your team is all iPhone, ServiceM8 is worth serious consideration. If not, Tradify is the lower-risk choice.

Against Powered Now: No contest on UK compliance. Powered Now handles Gas Safe certificates, Legionella templates, CIS, MTD, and offline working natively. ServiceM8 wins on client automation and forms flexibility. For any plumber with compliance requirements, Powered Now addresses requirements that ServiceM8 simply doesn't support.

The Tradesman's Math

On the Growing plan at ~£29/mo for 50 jobs: roughly 29 minutes of admin saved per week at a £50/hr rate to break even. The automated client communications alone — no-show reduction, faster payment from automated invoice follow-ups — realistically covers that for any plumber doing regular domestic call-out work. One Trustpilot reviewer reported saving 20–30 hours of admin per week, which is the high end but consistent with teams transitioning from fully paper-based coordination.

Who This Is NOT For

Any team with Android users. Unless you're prepared to standardise on iPhone — and factor in the device cost — ServiceM8's Android limitations mean field engineers won't have access to time tracking and other core features. The workarounds are frustrating according to multiple reviews.

Gas Safe registered plumbers needing CP12 or Gas Safety Records. These documents are not in ServiceM8. You'll need Powered Now or a separate certificate system running alongside it, which largely defeats the purpose of unified job management software.

Subcontractors working under CIS. CIS reverse charge invoicing requires manual configuration. Powered Now handles this properly.

High-volume operations where job count varies significantly month to month. The per-job pricing model makes cashflow forecasting harder. Tradify's flat rate is easier to budget around.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does ServiceM8 work on Android for plumbers?

ServiceM8 has an Android app but it's a lite version — time tracking is iOS-only, and several features work differently or don't appear on Android. If any team member uses Android, this is a serious operational constraint. One Capterra reviewer switched their entire team to iPhone to use ServiceM8 properly.

Can ServiceM8 produce Gas Safe certificates or CP12 records?

No. ServiceM8 cannot produce Gas Safety Records, CP12 landlord certificates, or any Gas Safe compliance documentation. It has no Legionella risk assessment templates either. For Gas Safe-registered plumbers, Powered Now is the only job management tool in this category with native compliance certificate capability.

How does ServiceM8 pricing work for plumbing businesses?

ServiceM8 charges per job completed per month, not per user. Plans start at ~£9/mo for 15 jobs and scale with volume. This is cheaper than per-user competitors at low job volumes but more expensive at high volumes. A busy month costs more than a quiet one, which affects cashflow forecasting.

Is ServiceM8 good for UK plumbers?

For iOS-only teams doing high-volume domestic plumbing without gas compliance requirements, yes — the client automation and custom forms are genuinely excellent. For anyone with Android users, Gas Safe compliance needs, or CIS work, it's the wrong tool. Tradify or Powered Now serve most UK plumbing businesses better.
