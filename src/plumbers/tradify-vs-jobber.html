Tradify vs Jobber: Which Is Better for UK Plumbers? (2026)

Quick Verdict

Tradify for most UK plumbing businesses under five people. The pricing gap is significant for small teams — a two-person team pays £68/mo on Tradify vs £134/mo on Jobber the moment you add a second user. Tradify's Xero integration is also cleaner, which matters for most UK plumbing businesses whose accountants work primarily in Xero.

Jobber becomes the better choice at five or more engineers where the Connect plan dilutes across the team, and for heating and plumbing maintenance businesses where the client portal — letting property managers approve quotes and track job history without calling the office — is worth real money in time saved and client retention. Neither tool produces Gas Safe certificates or handles CIS natively; for that, Powered Now is the right tool.

The Core Difference

Tradify is built around the field trades workflow — quote, schedule, invoice, sync to accounting. It's lean, fast to set up, and predictably priced. Jobber is built for growing service businesses and has invested heavily in the client experience layer — self-service portal, AI marketing, route optimisation — features that aren't relevant for a two-person domestic plumbing operation but become genuinely valuable for a larger heating and plumbing business managing multiple ongoing contracts.

The Pricing Gap

Jobber's Core plan at ~£31/mo looks competitive for a sole trader. The moment you add a second person, the price jumps to Connect at ~£134/mo — a £103/mo increase for one additional user. Tradify's model is straightforward: £34/mo per person. Two people = £68/mo.

Jobber's team plan only becomes cost-competitive when there are five or more users. At that point, the Connect plan covers up to five users for ~£134/mo — £26.80 per user compared to Tradify's £34/mo. The economics invert above five people. Below five people, Tradify is materially cheaper at almost every team size.

Where Each Tool Wins

Tradify wins on

  • Pricing for small teams. Significantly cheaper at 1–4 users.
  • Xero integration. Most reliable and automated in the category.
  • Quoting speed. Faster to build and send quotes from price lists.
  • UK-focused development. Better awareness of UK market needs.
  • Simpler setup. Lower complexity, faster to get operational.

Jobber wins on

  • Client portal. Self-service for property managers and commercial clients to approve jobs and pay invoices.
  • Scheduling at scale. Route optimisation and scheduling depth better for 5+ engineers.
  • AI marketing. Maintenance contract renewal campaigns, seasonal boiler service reminders.
  • Offline mode. Added Jan 2026 — functional for field use.

The Catches

Tradify: No client self-service portal. Reporting is shallow. No Gas Safe certificates. No offline mode. The email footer on every outbound message.

Jobber: Sharp pricing jump at two users. QuickBooks integration has documented line-item sync issues — test carefully if your accountant uses QuickBooks. USD pricing means GBP costs fluctuate with exchange rates. No Gas Safe certificates. No native CIS support. Google Calendar sync is 24-hour lag, not real-time.

Who Should Choose Which

Choose Tradify if

  • You have 1–4 engineers
  • Your accountant uses Xero
  • You want a simple, proven UK-market tool
  • Budget predictability matters

Choose Jobber if

  • You have 5+ engineers
  • You serve property managers who want a client portal
  • You run heating maintenance contracts and want AI marketing
  • Your accountant uses Xero (not QuickBooks)
