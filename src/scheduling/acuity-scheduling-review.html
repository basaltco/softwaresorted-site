If you sell more than one type of appointment — a therapist running individual and group sessions, a salon with several treatment lengths, a coach selling a package of calls rather than a single booking — Calendly's single-event-type simplicity starts to feel limiting. Acuity Scheduling is built for exactly this case, at the cost of a steeper setup than Calendly.

Quick Verdict

Acuity Scheduling is the right choice for UK service businesses with more than one offering — packages, memberships, varying appointment lengths, or staff-specific availability. It doesn't have a permanent free tier, so it's a bigger commitment than trying Calendly first, but the customisation depth on intake forms and package sales is the best of the four tools in this cluster. Budget a proper setup session rather than expecting it to work in five minutes out of the box.

A Real Workflow Example

A UK therapist setting up Acuity for a mixed practice: creates separate appointment types for "Initial Consultation" (60 minutes), "Follow-up Session" (50 minutes), and a "6-Session Package" sold upfront at a discount. Sets different buffer times for each, builds a custom intake form collecting relevant background information before the first session, and configures availability so package clients can only book follow-ups after completing the initial consultation. This kind of layered setup isn't possible on Calendly's simpler model — but it takes longer to configure than Calendly's 15-minute setup.

The Catch

Steeper learning curve than Calendly. Editorial software comparison sites commonly describe the process of configuring multiple appointment types, intake forms, and package pricing as more involved — not difficult, but not a five-minute job either. If your need is genuinely simple (one service, one duration, one price), Acuity's extra configuration options are overhead you don't need, and Calendly's free tier will get you there faster.

No permanent free tier. You're committing to a paid plan (after a trial) from the start, unlike Calendly or SimplyBook.me where a usable free option exists indefinitely.

GDPR and Data Residency

Acuity Scheduling is a US company (part of Squarespace), and customer data is stored on US servers. As with Calendly, this is lawful under Standard Contractual Clauses and a Data Processing Addendum, but the business using Acuity is responsible for having that DPA in place — particularly important if you're a therapist or coach collecting health or otherwise sensitive intake information. See our GDPR guide for UK scheduling software before building intake forms that collect sensitive data.

Who This Is NOT For

Anyone wanting the simplest possible setup — Calendly's free tier and faster configuration will suit a single-service freelancer better.

Businesses that want a free tier with no time pressure to commit — Acuity's trial-only model means you're choosing a paid plan from the outset.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Acuity Scheduling have a free plan?

No. Acuity offers a free trial but no permanent free tier, unlike Calendly and SimplyBook.me. After the trial, you'll need to choose a paid plan to continue using it.

Is Acuity Scheduling good for therapists or coaches running packages?

Yes — this is one of Acuity's strongest use cases. It supports selling session packages and memberships upfront, custom intake forms for client background information, and different appointment types with their own durations and buffer times.

Is Acuity Scheduling GDPR compliant for UK businesses?

Acuity, like Calendly, is a US company relying on Standard Contractual Clauses for lawful international data transfer. You're responsible as the business for having a Data Processing Addendum in place, especially if collecting sensitive intake information.

Can I sell packages or memberships through Acuity?

Yes, this is a core feature — package and membership sales with payment integration are better supported on Acuity than on Calendly, which has no native equivalent.

How long does it take to set up Acuity Scheduling?

Longer than Calendly. A single appointment type can be configured in a similar time frame, but the value of Acuity comes from setting up multiple appointment types, custom intake forms, and package pricing — which is realistically an hour or more of setup rather than 15 minutes.

What's the difference between Acuity and Calendly?

Calendly is simpler and has a usable free tier; Acuity has no permanent free tier but offers deeper customisation for multiple services, packages, and intake forms. See our Calendly vs Acuity comparison for the full breakdown.
