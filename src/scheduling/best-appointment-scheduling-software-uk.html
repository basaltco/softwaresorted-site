If you're a freelancer, therapist, coach, or consultant in the UK, you've probably looked at scheduling software and found the same problem — most guides are written for a US audience and bolt on a currency symbol. None of them mention GDPR, UK data residency, or what happens to your booking page if the tool you picked stores customer data outside the UK without a proper agreement in place.

This guide covers the four tools UK small businesses actually compare — Calendly, SimplyBook.me, Acuity Scheduling, and YouCanBook.me — based on UK pricing, GDPR documentation each vendor publishes, and patterns from verified Capterra, G2, and Trustpilot reviews. Doodle is mentioned for completeness, but it solves a different problem — finding a time for a group, not taking bookings — so it isn't reviewed alongside the other three.

Quick Verdict

Calendly is the best all-rounder for freelancers, consultants, and coaches who want fast setup and a generous free tier — but UK users should check the time zone handling before relying on it for client-facing bookings (see Catches). SimplyBook.me is the strongest choice if you take payments and want invoicing built in, with the most comprehensive built-in payment handling of the group, though costs climb fast once you switch on extra features. Acuity Scheduling suits service businesses who want deep customisation of intake forms and packages, and is happy to spend more setup time getting there. YouCanBook.me is the one to know about if you'd rather pay a UK-founded company — though pricing is USD, not GBP.

If you're a UK therapist, coach, or consultant and just want a recommendation: start with Calendly's free tier, and move to SimplyBook.me if you need to take payments at the point of booking.

Why This Decision Matters

At £0–£42/month, the subscription cost isn't the main risk — the main risk is picking a tool that creates a GDPR or client-trust problem six months in. Several of these tools store data on US servers. That isn't disqualifying on its own (Standard Contractual Clauses and a signed DPA make it lawful), but it means you need to actually check the vendor has UK/EU data-protection paperwork available — not just assume it because the website looks professional. This matters more for therapists and coaches handling sensitive client information than for a freelance designer booking discovery calls.

The other real cost is the one nobody mentions in scheduling-software roundups: feature-gated pricing. Several tools price their "starter" tier attractively, then each useful feature — calendar sync, online payments, SMS reminders — pushes you into a higher tier one feature at a time. Budget for the tier you'll actually need once notifications and payments are switched on, not the entry price.

GDPR and Data Residency

Calendly, Acuity, and YouCanBook.me are US-headquartered companies. SimplyBook.me is Iceland-headquartered (EEA, so GDPR-aligned by default). For the three US-based tools, UK/EU customer data is protected under Standard Contractual Clauses and a Data Processing Addendum (DPA) — but you, as the business using the tool, are responsible for having that DPA in place if you're collecting client data through your booking page. Each vendor publishes a DPA or GDPR page; none of them will proactively tell you to sign it.

This matters most if you're a therapist, coach, or any business collecting health, financial, or otherwise sensitive information at the point of booking — ICO guidance treats this as higher-risk processing. A dedicated breakdown of how each tool handles this is covered in our GDPR-compliant scheduling software guide — read that before committing if data protection is a live concern for your business.

The Four Tools Worth Your Time

Best for fast setup

Calendly — free tier worth using

Approx. pricing: Free tier available; Standard from approx. £8–10/month per user (USD pricing, converts with exchange rate — check calendly.com for current GBP-equivalent billing)

Calendly's free tier is genuinely usable for a solo freelancer or consultant — one event type, calendar sync, and automated confirmation emails, with no time limit on the free plan. Setup takes minutes: connect your calendar, set availability, share a link. For anyone whose main need is "let people book a slot in my diary without back-and-forth emails," this is the lowest-friction starting point of the four.

Catch: Multiple Trustpilot reviewers report time zone handling issues specific to UK users — bookings occasionally display in the wrong time zone for the invitee, which has caused missed meetings. Worth testing your own booking flow from a non-UK browser/device setting before relying on it for client-facing bookings.

Best for payments + invoicing

SimplyBook.me — built-in payment collection

Approx. pricing: Free (£0), Basic (£7.50/month), Standard (£20.90/month), Premium (£41.90/month) — GBP pricing confirmed direct from vendor

SimplyBook.me bundles online payment collection and invoicing into the booking flow itself, which Calendly and Acuity both require a third-party integration to achieve. For a business that wants to take a deposit or full payment at the point of booking — beauticians, tutors, consultants charging for first sessions — this removes a step that the other tools leave to Stripe or PayPal add-ons. Capterra UK rates it 4.6/5 across 1,281 reviews, the highest review volume of the group.

Catch: SimplyBook.me prices by "custom feature" — each one you switch on (Google Calendar sync, online payments, SMS reminders) consumes a feature slot, and you'll likely need the Standard tier rather than Basic once you've enabled the features most businesses actually want. Reviewers consistently flag this as the gap between the advertised entry price and what they ended up paying. Budget for Standard (£20.90/month), not Basic, when comparing real costs.

Best for service businesses with complex bookings

Acuity Scheduling — deep customisation

Approx. pricing: USD pricing, GBP-equivalent varies with exchange rate — check acuityscheduling.com for current rates. Three paid tiers, no perpetual free tier (free trial only).

Acuity's strength is customisable intake forms, package/membership sales, and the ability to set genuinely complex availability rules — different services with different durations, buffer times, and staff assignments. This suits service businesses with more than one offering (a therapist running individual and group sessions, a salon with multiple treatment types) better than Calendly's simpler single-event-type model.

Catch: Some reviewers describe the initial setup — particularly customising intake forms and package pricing — as more involved than Calendly's. Worth budgeting an actual setup session rather than expecting it to work in five minutes out of the box.

YouCanBook.me — UK-founded company

Approx. pricing: USD-denominated — no GBP-specific pricing published. Check youcanbook.me for current rates.

YouCanBook.me (YOUCANBOOKME LIMITED) is registered in England and Wales, founded in Bedford in 2010. For a UK buyer who'd rather their subscription money goes to a UK-domiciled company — relevant for some buyers on principle, and occasionally relevant for data-residency comfort — this is the only tool in the group with that profile.

Catch: Despite being UK-founded, there's no GBP-specific pricing — you're billed in USD, which means your monthly cost moves with the exchange rate. Functionally it covers similar ground to Calendly without a meaningfully different feature set, so the UK-company angle is the main reason to pick it over Calendly rather than a functionality gap.

Where Doodle Fits

Doodle solves a different problem: finding a time that works across several people's calendars for a meeting, not letting a client book a slot in your availability. If you need to schedule a group call rather than take bookings from customers, Doodle is the right category of tool — but it isn't a substitute for Calendly, SimplyBook.me, Acuity, or YouCanBook.me, and we don't review it as a competitor to them here.

Who This Is NOT For

This guide is for UK small businesses booking appointments with individual clients — therapists, coaches, consultants, freelancers, tutors, salon owners. It is not for:

UK tradespeople — electricians, plumbers, heating engineers need job costing, quoting, invoicing, and compliance certificates (CP12, EICR) bundled together. See our job management software guides instead — a general scheduling tool won't cover that workflow.

Enterprise/multi-location businesses needing complex resource and staff scheduling across teams and sites — these four tools are built for solo or small-team use.

Anyone whose primary need is a CRM — none of these tools are a substitute for a proper CRM if managing customer relationships, not just bookings, is the core requirement.

What These Tools Don't Do

None of these tools handle invoicing beyond the booking itself (except SimplyBook.me's built-in payment collection), VAT/accounting, or contract management. They manage the booking layer — calendar availability, confirmation, reminders, and (for SimplyBook.me) payment at the point of booking. For businesses that also need quoting, job tracking, and compliance paperwork, a job management tool is a better fit than a scheduling tool — see our trades-focused guides for that comparison.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Calendly GDPR compliant for UK businesses?

Calendly publishes a UK GDPR addendum and Standard Contractual Clauses for international data transfers, since customer data is stored on US servers. It's lawful to use, but you're responsible as the business for having a Data Processing Addendum in place if you're collecting client data through your Calendly booking page — this doesn't happen automatically. See our GDPR guide for the full breakdown across all four tools.

What's the cheapest scheduling software for a UK small business?

Calendly and SimplyBook.me both have usable free tiers. Calendly's free tier suits a single event type with no payment collection; SimplyBook.me's free tier is more limited but includes basic payment collection. Once you need calendar sync, reminders, and payments together, budget for SimplyBook.me's Standard tier (£20.90/month) rather than the free tier.

Can I take payments through these scheduling tools?

SimplyBook.me has payment collection and invoicing built in natively. Calendly and Acuity Scheduling require a Stripe or PayPal integration to collect payment at the point of booking. YouCanBook.me's payment capability should be confirmed directly with the vendor before relying on it.

Which scheduling software is best for therapists or coaches in the UK?

Acuity Scheduling's package and membership features suit therapists running structured session packages. SimplyBook.me suits therapists who want to collect payment at the point of booking. Given the sensitivity of client health information, read the GDPR guide before choosing either — DPA and data residency matter more for this use case than for a general freelancer.

What's the difference between Calendly and Doodle?

Calendly lets clients book an open slot in your availability — it's a booking tool. Doodle finds a time that works across multiple people's calendars for a meeting — it's a poll/coordination tool. They solve different problems and aren't direct alternatives to each other.

Do I need a credit card to start a free trial?

Calendly's free tier requires no card and has no time limit. SimplyBook.me's free tier also requires no card. Acuity Scheduling and YouCanBook.me offer free trials rather than a permanent free tier — check current trial terms directly with each vendor, as these change.
