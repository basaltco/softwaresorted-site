If you've ever sent three emails back and forth just to agree a time for a call, you already understand why Calendly exists. It's the most recognised name in appointment scheduling, and for a freelancer, consultant, or coach in the UK, the appeal is obvious — but the GDPR question and a documented time zone quirk are worth understanding before you build your booking process around it.

Quick Verdict

Calendly is the right choice if you want a free, fast way to let clients book a slot in your calendar without back-and-forth emails. It's the simplest of the four tools in this cluster to set up, and the free tier is genuinely usable rather than a stripped-down trial. The catch: test your booking flow from a UK browser and device setting before relying on it for client-facing meetings — UK users have reported time zone display issues, and a missed or mistimed meeting is a worse outcome than five minutes of testing would have prevented.

A Real Workflow Example

A freelance copywriter setting up Calendly for discovery calls: connect Google Calendar, create a 30-minute "Discovery Call" event type, set a 15-minute buffer either side so back-to-back calls don't run into each other, and add two qualifying questions to the booking form (budget range, project timeline). Share the link in an email signature and on a "Work With Me" page. Total setup time: under 15 minutes, no payment required to start.

The Catch

Time zone display issues. Multiple Trustpilot reviewers report time zone handling issues specific to UK users — bookings have displayed in the wrong time zone for the invitee, which has caused missed meetings. This appears to be an edge case rather than the norm, but it's a documented pattern worth testing for yourself: book a test slot from a device set to a different time zone and confirm the confirmation email shows the correct UK time before you put the link in front of real clients.

Single event type on the free tier. Calendly's free tier is limited to one event type, which is fine for a single service offering but restrictive the moment you offer more than one type of meeting.

No native payment collection. If you need to take a deposit or fee at the point of booking, you'll need to connect Stripe or PayPal separately, or look at SimplyBook.me instead.

GDPR and Data Residency

Calendly is a US company, and customer data is stored on US servers. Calendly publishes a UK GDPR addendum and relies on Standard Contractual Clauses for international data transfers, which makes it lawful to use — but the responsibility for having a Data Processing Addendum in place sits with you, the business, not with Calendly automatically. This matters more if you're collecting sensitive information (health, financial, or other special-category data) at the point of booking. Who This Is NOT For

Businesses that need to take payment at the point of booking — Calendly requires a separate Stripe/PayPal integration; SimplyBook.me has this built in natively.

Businesses offering several distinct services or package deals — Calendly's single-event-type model on the free tier, and even paid tiers, is less flexible than Acuity Scheduling for this.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Calendly free to use for a UK business?

Yes — Calendly's free tier includes one event type, calendar sync, and automated confirmation emails, with no time limit. It's a genuinely usable starting point for a solo freelancer or consultant, not a stripped-down trial.

Is Calendly GDPR compliant for UK users?

Calendly provides a UK GDPR addendum and Standard Contractual Clauses to make international data transfer lawful, since data is stored on US servers. As the business using it, you're responsible for having a Data Processing Addendum in place if you collect client data through your booking page.

Does Calendly support recurring bookings, like a weekly client slot?

Calendly supports round-robin and recurring event types on paid tiers, allowing a client to book a regular weekly or fortnightly slot. The free tier is limited to single, non-recurring event types.

Can clients pay when they book through Calendly?

Not natively. Calendly integrates with Stripe and PayPal so you can collect payment at the point of booking, but this requires setting up a separate payment account and connecting it — it isn't built into Calendly itself the way it is with SimplyBook.me.

Why does Calendly show the wrong time zone for some UK clients?

This has been reported by multiple Trustpilot reviewers as an intermittent display issue rather than a constant fault. Test your own booking link from a device set to a different time zone before relying on it for client-facing meetings, to confirm the time shown is correct.

What's the difference between Calendly and Acuity Scheduling?

Calendly is simpler to set up and has a usable free tier; Acuity Scheduling has no permanent free tier but offers deeper customisation for businesses selling packages, memberships, or multiple service types. See our Calendly vs Acuity comparison for the full breakdown.
