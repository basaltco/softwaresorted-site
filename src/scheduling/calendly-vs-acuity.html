Calendly vs Acuity Scheduling: Which Is Right for Your UK Business? (2026)

Most "Calendly vs Acuity" comparisons online are written by a competitor of one or both tools — Zeeg, Koalendar, and Cal.com all have a commercial reason to steer you a particular way. This comparison has no affiliate relationship with either tool, so the recommendation depends entirely on what your business actually needs: simplicity, or customisation.

Quick Verdict

Choose Calendly if you offer one type of appointment and want the fastest possible setup with a usable free tier. Choose Acuity Scheduling if you offer multiple services, sell packages or memberships, or need detailed custom intake forms — Acuity has no permanent free tier, but the configuration depth justifies the extra setup time for a more complex booking need.

Head-to-Head by Use Case

  • Solo freelancer, one type of call or meeting → Calendly. Faster setup, free tier covers it.
  • Therapist or coach selling session packages → Acuity. Native package sales beat Calendly's single-event model.
  • Salon or clinic with multiple treatment types and durations → Acuity. Better suited to varied appointment configuration.
  • Want to try before paying anything → Calendly. Acuity has no permanent free tier.

The Catches, Side by Side

Calendly's catch: Multiple Trustpilot reviewers report UK time zone display issues — test your booking flow before relying on it for client-facing meetings. The free tier is limited to one event type.

Acuity's catch: No permanent free tier, and the setup for multiple appointment types and intake forms takes longer than Calendly's. Editorial comparison sites commonly describe this as a steeper initial learning curve.

GDPR Note

Both Calendly and Acuity are US companies relying on Standard Contractual Clauses for lawful data transfer — neither has a structural GDPR advantage over the other. Frequently Asked Questions

Which is cheaper, Calendly or Acuity Scheduling?

Calendly has a usable free tier, making it cheaper to start with. Acuity has no permanent free tier — only a trial — so it requires committing to a paid plan sooner.

Which has a free tier, Calendly or Acuity?

Calendly does, with one event type and core booking features. Acuity offers a free trial only.

Which is better for selling packages or memberships?

Acuity Scheduling. Native package and membership sales with payment integration is one of its core strengths, and not something Calendly offers natively.

Which is easier to set up?

Calendly — a single event type can be live in around 15 minutes. Acuity's value comes from configuring multiple appointment types and intake forms, which takes longer.

Which is more GDPR-friendly for UK businesses?

Neither has a structural advantage — both are US companies relying on Standard Contractual Clauses. SimplyBook.me's EEA headquarters gives it a more straightforward default GDPR position if that's your priority.
