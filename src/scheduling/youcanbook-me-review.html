If it matters to you that your subscription money goes to a UK-founded company, YouCanBook.me is the one tool in this comparison that fits — but the pricing is in US dollars, not pounds, so the UK-company angle is more about principle than practical advantage.

Quick Verdict

YouCanBook.me does a similar job to Calendly — booking pages, calendar sync, automated confirmations — without a meaningfully different feature set. The main reason to choose it over Calendly is that it's a UK-incorporated company (YOUCANBOOKME LIMITED, founded in Bedford in 2010), which may matter to you on principle or for data-residency comfort. Worth knowing: YouCanBook.me was acquired by Capacity, a US AI company, in 2025 — if data residency is a deciding factor for your business, confirm YouCanBook.me's current data handling directly with them rather than relying on its UK incorporation history alone.

A Real Workflow Example

A UK-based virtual assistant setting up YouCanBook.me for client onboarding calls: connects calendar, creates a single booking page for "30-Minute Onboarding Call," sets working hours and buffer time, and embeds the booking widget on their website rather than just sharing a link. The day-to-day workflow is comparable to setting up Calendly — the main practical difference shows up on the invoice, which arrives in USD.

The Catch

No GBP-specific pricing. You're billed in USD, which means your monthly cost moves with the exchange rate — the opposite of what you'd expect from a UK-founded tool. Functionally, YouCanBook.me covers similar ground to Calendly without a clearly differentiated feature set, so the case for choosing it is mostly about vendor preference rather than capability.

Frequently Asked Questions

No. Pricing is USD-denominated, so UK customers are billed in US dollars and the GBP cost moves with the exchange rate.

Is YouCanBook.me GDPR compliant?

YouCanBook.me's UK founding doesn't by itself confirm current data residency, especially following its 2025 acquisition by a US company. Confirm directly with YouCanBook.me where data is stored and whether a Data Processing Addendum is available before relying on it for sensitive client information.

What happened when Capacity acquired YouCanBook.me?

Capacity, a US AI-driven customer support company, acquired YouCanBook.me in 2025. The practical impact on infrastructure and data handling isn't detailed publicly — if this matters to your decision, ask YouCanBook.me directly rather than assuming continuity with its UK-founded history.
